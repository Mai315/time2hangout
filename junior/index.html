Welcome to Our Junior Program

Our junior program is designed for children aged five and above, focusing on balance, coordination, throttle control, and safe riding habits. All sessions are conducted in a controlled environment with child-appropriate bikes, full protective gear, close supervision, and a parent/guardian required to be present.

We believe in creating confident, skilled young riders through fun, structured, and safety-first coaching. Our program emphasizes outdoor lifestyle, personal development, and a love for adventure—all within a safe and supportive environment.

Student Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age of 5 years old
  • Able to balance a bicycle independently
  • Able to follow simple instructions and safety rules

Parental and Guardian Requirements

  • A parent or guardian must remain on-site during all lessons
  • Mandatory parent-child orientation session during first lesson
  • Parents receive regular progress updates on their child's development

Safety and Equipment Standards

  • Full protective gear provided: Helmet, gloves, knee and elbow guards
  • Small class sizes with maximum ratio of 1 coach to 4 students
  • All instructors are trained in child handling, first-aid, and CPR
  • Child-appropriate bikes suited to rider size and skill level
  • Pre-ride safety checks performed before every session

Program Details

  • Teaching Style: Positive reinforcement with playful learning format
  • Curriculum Focus: Balance, throttle control, braking, standing posture, safe stopping, and confidence building
  • Session Duration: 60-120 minutes (age-appropriate lengths)
  • Progress Tracking: Simple skill milestones with parent updates

Our 5-Stage Training Program

Stage 1 – Introduction and Foundation Skills

Build comfort, safety awareness, and control basics

  • Bike introduction and controls familiarization
  • Proper off-road body position and stance
  • Clutch and throttle coordination
  • Slow-speed balance and brake control

Stage 2 – Terrain Handling and Confidence Building

Develop control and reaction skills on variable surfaces

  • Cornering technique on dirt and gravel
  • Weight shifting and counter-steering
  • Front and rear brake coordination
  • Riding over ruts, bumps, and slopes

Stage 3 – Obstacle Navigation and Intermediate Skills

Learn to handle real off-road conditions safely

  • Line selection and vision technique
  • Handling mud, sand, and uneven tracks
  • Lifting a fallen bike safely
  • Controlled jumps and whoop sections (introductory level)

Stage 4 – Advanced Techniques and Speed Control

Sharpen reaction timing and precision control

  • Clutch control for traction management
  • Advanced cornering and braking control
  • Power delivery on hill climbs
  • Group riding and overtaking etiquette

Stage 5 – Track Integration and Assessment

Combine all skills into real-track riding scenarios

  • Full-lap riding under supervision
  • Race-start techniques and line practice
  • Emergency recovery and safety drills
  • Performance evaluation with Certificate of Completion
